What To Do In An Emergency

Follow these steps calmly and in order to get help as fast as possible.

1

Stay Calm

Take a breath. Panicking can delay help. Assess the situation quickly but carefully before taking action.

2

Call for Help

Dial the Campus Clinic at (082) 123-4567 or Campus Security at (082) 765-4321 immediately.

3

Give Your Location

Clearly state your building name, floor, and room number so responders can reach you without delay.

4

Do Not Move the Injured

Unless there is immediate danger (e.g., fire), avoid moving an injured person to prevent further harm.

5

Keep the Area Clear

Ask bystanders to step back. Keep the path clear for emergency responders to access the scene.

6

Wait & Assist

Stay on the line with the responder, follow their instructions, and send someone to guide help to your location.

First Aid Quick Reference

Basic steps to take while waiting for professional help to arrive.

Apply firm, direct pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or bandage. Do not remove the cloth if it soaks through — add more on top. Elevate the injured limb above heart level if possible. Seek medical attention if bleeding does not slow within 10 minutes.

Call 911 immediately. Check for responsiveness and breathing. If unresponsive, begin CPR: 30 chest compressions (hard and fast, center of chest) followed by 2 rescue breaths. Continue until help arrives or the person regains consciousness. Use an AED if available.

Move the person to a cool, shaded area. Loosen tight clothing and apply cool (not ice cold) wet cloths to the skin. Have them lie down with legs slightly elevated if they fainted. Give water in small sips if they are conscious. Call for help if they do not recover quickly.

Stay with the person and time the seizure. Clear the area of hard or sharp objects. Cushion their head with something soft. Do NOT restrain them or put anything in their mouth. After the seizure, turn them gently on their side. Call for help if the seizure lasts more than 5 minutes.

Ask "Are you choking?" If they cannot speak or breathe, perform the Heimlich maneuver: stand behind them, give 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades, then 5 abdominal thrusts just above the navel. Alternate until the object is dislodged or help arrives. Call 911 immediately.
